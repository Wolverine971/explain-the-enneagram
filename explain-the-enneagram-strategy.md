# Explain the Enneagram — Domain Strategy & Content Guide

## Purpose

This document defines the content strategy, positioning, and cross-linking rules for the "Explain the Enneagram" domain. It serves as the canonical reference for anyone (human or AI) working on this property.

---

## Positioning

**Explain the Enneagram = How to teach, explain, and apply the Enneagram professionally.**

This is NOT another Enneagram educational blog. The companion domain (9takes.com) already has 84+ published Enneagram articles covering beginner through advanced topics. Duplicating that content cannibalizes search rankings and wastes effort.

Instead, this domain targets a distinct audience with distinct intent:

| | Explain the Enneagram | 9takes.com |
|---|---|---|
| **Audience** | Coaches, therapists, facilitators, HR professionals, teachers | Individuals exploring their own personality |
| **Intent** | "How do I explain/teach/apply this?" | "What does this mean for me?" |
| **Voice** | Practitioner-facing, instructional, framework-oriented | Community-driven, personal, exploratory |
| **Content type** | Scripts, frameworks, facilitation guides, case studies | Q&A threads, blog posts, celebrity analyses |
| **Value prop** | Make you better at using the Enneagram with others | Help you understand yourself and others |

### One-liner

> Explain the Enneagram helps practitioners teach, coach, and facilitate the Enneagram with confidence.

## Content Tiers

All content should answer some version of: "How do I explain/teach/use this concept with a client, team, or audience?"

### Tier 1 — Foundations (101)

Content for practitioners who are new to teaching the Enneagram. Self-contained articles that don't require linking out.

**Examples:**
- How to explain the Enneagram in 5 minutes (elevator pitch for coaches)
- How to explain each type without stereotyping
- The 3 most common mistakes new Enneagram coaches make
- How to introduce the Enneagram to a skeptic
- Explaining the Enneagram without religious baggage
- How to explain wings without overwhelming beginners
- Quick-reference type summaries for workshop handouts

**Cross-link to 9takes:** Rarely. These are self-contained entry points.

### Tier 2 — Practitioner Frameworks (201)

Content for coaches and facilitators who know the basics and want structured approaches for sessions and workshops.

**Examples:**
- Workshop framework: Teaching subtypes in a group setting
- Client intake conversation templates by type
- How to structure a 6-week Enneagram coaching program
- Facilitating type panels: setup, questions, and debrief guide
- Teaching stress and growth arrows: visual aids and exercises
- How to explain compatibility without creating boxes
- Group exercise: mapping team dynamics using the Enneagram
- Coaching scripts for each type's core fear and desire
- How to debrief an Enneagram assessment with a client

**Cross-link to 9takes:** Yes — "See how real Type 4s describe their experience [link to 9takes question thread or type page]" when it genuinely enriches the practitioner's understanding.

### Tier 3 — Advanced Integration (301)

Content for therapists, organizational consultants, and experienced coaches integrating the Enneagram with other frameworks.

**Examples:**
- Integrating the Enneagram with CBT: a therapist's guide
- Enneagram meets ACT: acceptance strategies by type
- Using the Enneagram in IFS (Internal Family Systems) therapy
- Enneagram and attachment theory: a clinical framework
- Organizational development: Enneagram-based team assessments
- Trauma-informed Enneagram coaching: what to do and what to avoid
- The Enneagram in couples therapy: facilitator guide
- Enneagram and leadership development programs
- Research review: what the evidence says about the Enneagram

**Cross-link to 9takes:** Yes — "See how types describe their therapy experience" or "See real community responses about stress patterns" when linking to genuinely relevant 9takes content.

### Tier 4 — Tools & Resources

Practical downloadable/reference content that drives return visits and email signups.

**Examples:**
- Enneagram coach certification comparison guide (updated annually)
- Recommended books by practitioner level
- Printable type cards for workshops
- Assessment tool comparison for professional use
- Glossary of Enneagram terms for client-facing materials

**Cross-link to 9takes:** Minimal. Link only where 9takes has a genuinely relevant resource (e.g., the test comparison post).

## Cross-Linking Rules

These rules exist to maintain SEO safety and avoid Google penalties. Follow them exactly.

### DO

- **Disclose the relationship.** The about page must state: "From the creator of 9takes.com" or similar. Transparency removes the hidden-network concern.
- **Link contextually.** Every cross-link must live inside article body text, placed where a reader would genuinely benefit from clicking.
- **Vary anchor text.** Use a mix of branded ("9takes"), descriptive ("see real Type 5 answers"), and natural phrasing. Never repeat the same anchor across articles.
- **Link to third parties generously.** Every article that links to 9takes should also link to at least 2-3 other external sources (Enneagram Institute, Truity, academic papers, books, other coaching sites). This signals "real resource site," not "link funnel."
- **Keep it sparse.** Maximum 1-2 links to 9takes per article. Most articles should have zero.
- **Link bidirectionally but asymmetrically.** Some 9takes articles (like the coach toolkit post) can link back to this domain. This makes the relationship look mutual and editorial.

### DO NOT

- Put 9takes links in the header, footer, sidebar, or any sitewide element.
- Use exact-match keyword anchors like "Enneagram personality platform" or "personality-based Q&A."
- Link to 9takes from every article. Many articles should have no cross-links at all.
- Duplicate content topics that already exist on 9takes (see "Content That Already Exists on 9takes" section below).
- Launch with heavy cross-linking from day one. Build independent authority for 6-12 months first.

### Link patterns that work

```
Good: "To see how real Type 3s talk about their relationship with achievement,
       check out the community responses on 9takes."

Good: "9takes has a detailed breakdown of all 81 type pairings that's worth
       bookmarking as a reference."

Bad:  "Learn more about Enneagram types at 9takes.com, the leading
       Enneagram personality platform."

Bad:  "Visit 9takes for more Enneagram content." (every article footer)
```

## Building Independent Authority

Before this domain becomes a meaningful backlink source for 9takes, it needs its own authority. Target these independently:

### Backlink sources to pursue
- Enneagram coaching certification program directories
- Psychology Today contributor profiles or directory listings
- Coaching association resource pages (ICF, NBHWC)
- Guest posts on therapy/coaching blogs
- Podcast appearances on coaching and personal development shows
- Enneagram community forums and groups (genuine participation, not link drops)

### Authority signals to build
- Author bios with real credentials
- Citations to published research and books
- Original frameworks and methodologies (not just summaries)
- Email list with genuine engagement metrics
- Social proof from practitioners using the content

### Timeline
- **Months 1-6:** Publish foundational content, build email list, pursue third-party backlinks. Cross-link to 9takes sparingly (1-2 articles max).
- **Months 7-12:** Expand to Tier 2-3 content, increase cross-linking gradually as the domain builds its own search traffic.
- **Month 12+:** The domain should have its own organic traffic before being treated as a significant link source.

## Email Course Integration

The existing email course ("explain the Enneagram to coaching clients") fits naturally as a lead magnet. Reframe it:

- **Current:** "Learn the Enneagram" (too generic, competes with 9takes)
- **Reframed:** "The Practitioner's Guide to Explaining the Enneagram" or "Teach the Enneagram with Confidence: A 7-Day Email Course"

The email course should:
- Drive signups from Tier 1 and Tier 2 articles
- Deliver condensed versions of the workshop frameworks and coaching scripts
- Include 1-2 natural mentions of 9takes as a resource ("See real examples from typed users at 9takes.com") — not in every email
- Graduate subscribers toward Tier 3 content and any future paid offerings

## Technical Notes

- **Hosting:** Use different hosting infrastructure from 9takes if possible. Shared hosting is a signal Google uses to identify related domains.
- **Analytics:** Separate Google Analytics / Search Console properties. Track cross-domain traffic flows.
- **Schema markup:** Use `Organization` schema with the real business entity. Don't try to make them look unrelated.
- **Sitemap:** Standard XML sitemap submitted to Search Console independently.

## Summary

| Principle | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Positioning** | Practitioner-facing, not individual-facing |
| **Content** | "How to teach/apply X" not "What is X" |
| **Cross-links** | Contextual, sparse, varied anchors, never sitewide |
| **Third-party links** | Every article links to 2-3 external sources minimum |
| **Authority** | Build independently for 6-12 months before relying on cross-links |
| **Disclosure** | Publicly state the relationship with 9takes |
| **No duplication** | Never target the same search intent as existing 9takes content |
